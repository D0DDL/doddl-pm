Just starting out

Every journey begins somewhere

You would love your child to start learning how to use cutlery. They might not be able to hold a spoon yet, and that's completely fine. This is where we begin — together. We would recommend 'just starting out' products if all the following applies to your child:

Signs your child might be here
  • Your child has no experience of using cutlery
  • Your child has limited hand eye coordination
  • Your child may need some support to sit at mealtimes
  • Your child has involuntary movements
Every single one of these is worth celebrating. The interest is there — and that's everything. The rest follows.

When you start to see a grip — that's the next moment

When your child can hold the utensils, and move some food successfully to their mouth, you may wish to look at the next stage 'Establishing their grip'

Finding their grip

They're holding on — and that's huge

Your child is beginning to grip and guide. It might be inconsistent. It might take ten attempts to get a spoonful in. That's not failure — that's exactly what progress looks like at this stage - a desire to keep practising and celebrate the small wins. We would recommend doddl in this stage if the following apply:

Signs your child might be here
  • Your child can move food to their mouth
  • Your child has some experience of handling cutlery
  • Your child's appetite is growing
  • Your child is showing signs of wanting to self feed
The frustration means they want to do it themselves. That drive is everything. doddl is built to make those attempts successful more often.

Confidence comes in its own time

Once your child is regularly having successful mealtimes, and able to effectively scoop with the spoon and stab with the fork and no longer requires a suction bowl or plate, that's the sign they are ready to move onto 'building confidence' It doesn't happen overnight. And it doesn't need to.

Building confidence

More good days than hard ones

Learning to self-feed is coming on leaps and bounds. There may still be difficult mealtimes - that's normal, even expected. The tools at this stage are embracing their developing skills and making the good days more consistent, not eliminating the hard ones. Signs your child may be ready for 'building confidence':

Signs your child might be here
  • Self-feeding for part of their meal most days
  • Better days becoming more frequent than harder ones
  • Able to use a spoon and/or a fork effectively
  • Starting to show an interest in chopping their own food
The hard days don't erase the good ones. Every meal they feed themselves — even one spoonful — is real progress. Write it down if it helps.

Independence — when it arrives, you'll know

There isn't a checklist for this one. When your child sits down and just…starts eating — without so much help, without the tools slipping, with much less frustration — that's the moment. And it will come.

📝
Final stage — celebratory tone, not triumphant. We celebrate this loudly, but gently. Some families will be emotional reaching this point. The language should hold space for that. "Whatever age this happens" is the most important phrase on the page.
Eating independently 🎉

They did it. Whatever age. However long it took.

Your child is feeding themselves. Not sometimes — consistently, confidently, their own way. This is a milestone that many families were told might not come. And it came.

Signs your child is here
  • Self-feeding most or all of every meal
  • Comfortable with the tools — they feel natural now
  • Ready to try new foods, new textures, new challenges
  • Mealtimes have changed for the whole family
This took longer, required more patience, and meant more than most people around you will ever understand. You got here. Your child got here. That matters enormously.
