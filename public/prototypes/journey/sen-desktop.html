Just starting out

Every journey begins somewhere

Your child is starting to show interest in mealtimes. They might not be able to hold a spoon yet, and that's completely fine. This is where we begin — together.

Signs your child might be here
  • Starting to watch you eat and showing curiosity
  • Attempting to reach toward food or utensils
  • Some hand-to-mouth movement, even if unsteady
  • Sitting with support during mealtimes
Every single one of these is worth celebrating. The interest is there — and that's everything. The rest follows.

When you start to see a grip — that's the next moment

When your child begins holding the spoon rather than just reaching for it, even briefly, even imperfectly — that's the sign they're moving into the next chapter. There's no rush. You'll know when you see it.

Finding their grip

They're holding on — and that's huge

Your child is beginning to grip and guide. It might be inconsistent. It might take ten attempts to get a spoonful. That's not failure — that's exactly what progress looks like at this stage.

Signs your child might be here
  • Grasping the spoon or fork, even if loosely
  • Attempting to guide food toward their mouth
  • More deliberate reaching than before
  • Getting frustrated when they can't quite manage — a great sign of intent
The frustration means they want to do it themselves. That drive is everything. doddl is built to make those attempts successful more often.

Confidence comes in its own time

When your child starts having more successful meals than difficult ones — even if it's two good days then a harder one — that's the shift toward building confidence. It doesn't happen overnight. And it doesn't need to.

Building confidence

More good days than hard ones

Your child is self-feeding some of the time. There are still difficult meals — that's normal, even expected. The tools at this stage are about making the good days more consistent, not eliminating the hard ones.

Signs your child might be here
  • Self-feeding for part of the meal most days
  • Better days becoming more frequent than harder ones
  • Starting to show preferences — this food, that utensil
  • Less frustration with the tools, more with the food itself (progress!)
The hard days don't erase the good ones. Every meal they feed themselves — even one spoonful — is real progress. Write it down if it helps.

Independence — when it arrives, you'll know

There isn't a checklist for this one. When your child sits down and just… gets on with it — without you needing to help, without the tools slipping — that's the moment. And it will come.

📝
Final stage — celebratory tone, not triumphant. We celebrate this loudly, but gently. Some families will be emotional reaching this point. The language should hold space for that. "Whatever age this happens" is the most important phrase on the page.
Eating independently 🎉

They did it. Whatever age. However long it took.

Your child is feeding themselves. Not sometimes — consistently, confidently, their own way. This is a milestone that many families were told might not come. And it came.

Signs your child is here
  • Self-feeding most or all of every meal
  • Comfortable with the tools — they feel natural now
  • Ready to try new foods, new textures, new challenges
  • Mealtimes have changed for the whole family
This took longer, required more patience, and meant more than most people around you will ever understand. You got here. Your child got here. That matters enormously.
