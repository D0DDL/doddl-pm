Just starting out

Every journey begins somewhere

Your child is starting to show interest in mealtimes. They might not be able to hold a spoon yet — and that's completely fine.

Signs your child might be here
  • Starting to watch you eat and showing curiosity
  • Attempting to reach toward food or utensils
  • Some hand-to-mouth movement, even if unsteady
  • Sitting with support during mealtimes
Every single one of these is worth celebrating. The interest is there — and that's everything.

When you start to see a grip — that's the next moment

When your child begins holding the spoon rather than just reaching for it, even briefly — that's the sign. There's no rush.

Finding their grip

They're holding on — and that's huge

Your child is beginning to grip and guide. It might be inconsistent. Ten attempts for one spoonful. That's not failure — that's exactly what progress looks like.

Signs your child might be here
  • Grasping the spoon or fork, even if loosely
  • Attempting to guide food toward their mouth
  • More deliberate reaching than before
  • Frustration when they can't manage — a great sign of intent
The frustration means they want to do it themselves. That drive is everything.

Confidence comes in its own time

When your child has more successful meals than difficult ones — even if it's two good days then a harder one — that's the shift toward building confidence.

Building confidence

More good days than hard ones

Your child is self-feeding some of the time. There are still difficult meals — that's normal, even expected. The tools at this stage are about making the good days more consistent.

Signs your child might be here
  • Self-feeding for part of the meal most days
  • Better days becoming more frequent
  • Starting to show preferences — this food, that utensil
  • Less frustration with the tools, more with the food itself
The hard days don't erase the good ones. Every meal they feed themselves — even one spoonful — is real progress.

Independence — when it arrives, you'll know

When your child sits down and just gets on with it — without help, without the tools slipping — that's the moment. And it will come.

Eating independently 🎉

They did it. Whatever age. However long it took.

Your child is feeding themselves. Consistently. Confidently. Their own way. This is a milestone many families were told might not come. And it came.

Signs your child is here
  • Self-feeding most or all of every meal
  • Comfortable with the tools — they feel natural now
  • Ready to try new foods and new challenges
  • Mealtimes have changed for the whole family
This took longer, required more patience, and meant more than most people around you will ever understand. You got here.
