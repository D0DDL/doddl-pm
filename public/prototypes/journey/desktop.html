Coming up: Stage 1 — First Foods (6–12 months)

Your baby will be ready for their first spoon sooner than you think. Here's a preview of what Stage 1 looks like.

Stage 1 · 6–12 months

First foods, first wins

  • Sitting in a high chair with support
  • Reaching and grasping objects reliably
  • Bringing hand to mouth confidently
  • Showing real interest in what you eat

Ready for Stage 2? Signs to look for

When your child starts grabbing the spoon and trying to steer it themselves, they're ready for Stage 2 — Building Control (12–18m).

Stage 2 · 12–18 months

Grip it, own it

The pincer grip arrives and suddenly your toddler wants to do everything themselves. The right tools make that possible — and a lot less messy.

  • Pincer grip developing fast
  • Deliberately scooping food
  • Self-feeding for most of the meal
  • Frustrated if you try to help!

When your child starts wanting to cut food themselves or watches you use a knife with real intent, Stage 4 is next.

  • Ready to learn how to cut food safely
  • Copying adult eating patterns at the table
  • Fine motor control fully established
  • Interested in helping prepare food
